Background Info

Kameryn Louise "Kami" Craig was an American water polo player, playing the 2-meter/hole set position. She is one of the most decorated water polo athletes ever, winning two Olympic gold medals in 2012 and 2016, and winning a silver medal in 2008, all with the US. During her 4-year college career at USC, she won the Peter J. Cutino award twice, which is given to the top collegiate water polo player every year. A rock-solid point defender and an absolute beast of an offensive set player, Craig's offensive prowess emitted a certain "gravity" effect on the oppostion - if the defenders positioned themselves too far away from her, Craig would have had too easy of a time taking a wet pass from one of her teammates and scoring. This in turn created more space around the perimeter of the US women's (♀) National Water Polo Team's offense, allowing for more fluid ball movement and more efficient shot opportunities.
